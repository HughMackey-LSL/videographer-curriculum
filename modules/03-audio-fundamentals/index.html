Audio Fundamentals

Prioritize clean dialogue and usable sound — poor audio can make otherwise acceptable footage unusable.

What You'll Be Able to Do

  • Record clean interview audio.
  • Set and monitor appropriate audio levels.
  • Place lavalier and shotgun microphones effectively.
  • Troubleshoot common audio problems in the field.
  • Capture acceptable event audio or recognize when additional support is needed.

Module Assignment

Record the same 60-second interview segment twice — once with a lavalier microphone and once with a shotgun microphone. Listen back with headphones and write brief notes on what you hear differently in each recording.
